OpenAI is [expanding its Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program](https://openai.com/index/scaling-trusted-access-for-cyber-defense/) to thousands of verified individual defenders and hundreds of organizations, and introducing **GPT-5.4-Cyber** — a variant of GPT-5.4 fine-tuned to be cyber-permissive. The model lowers refusal boundaries for defensive work and adds capabilities such as binary reverse engineering, letting security professionals analyze compiled software for vulnerabilities without source access. Because it is more permissive, GPT-5.4-Cyber ships only to vetted vendors, organizations, and researchers, with tighter deployment controls; access to features like Zero-Data Retention may be restricted. Individuals can verify at `chatgpt.com/cyber`; enterprises go through an OpenAI representative.

The move lands one week after Anthropic's Project Glasswing and [Claude Mythos](https://cyberscoop.com/openai-expands-trusted-access-for-cyber-to-thousands-for-cybersecurity/), and OpenAI frames its approach around three principles: democratized access, iterative deployment, and ecosystem resilience. The company ties the release to ongoing defensive investments — a $10M Cybersecurity Grant Program, Codex for Open Source reaching over 1,000 projects, and Codex Security contributing to 3,000+ critical and high-severity fixes since its recent launch. GPT-5.4 itself is already classified as "high" cyber capability under OpenAI's Preparedness Framework, so the cyber-tuned variant sits at the permissive end of that bar.

Key points:

- **GPT-5.4-Cyber** — fine-tuned on GPT-5.4 with reduced refusals for defensive work and added binary reverse-engineering capability
- **Tiered TAC access** — verified individuals get reduced-friction versions of existing models; highest tier gates GPT-5.4-Cyber for vetted defenders only
- **Deployment limits** — cyber-permissive model comes with restrictions, potentially including limits on Zero-Data Retention
- **Positioning vs. Anthropic** — follows Claude Mythos by about a week; OpenAI avoids direct comparison and emphasizes verification-based access over central gatekeeping
- **Ecosystem signal** — framed as part of ongoing defender support: $10M grants, 1,000+ open-source projects scanned, 3,000+ high/critical fixes via Codex Security
