Anthropic has become the first major AI chatbot provider to require government ID verification — passports, driver's licenses, or national ID cards — from users flagged for "potentially fraudulent or abusive behavior."

The timing is awkward. In February 2026, Claude saw a [60% surge in free signups](https://decrypt.co/364509/claude-anthropic-government-id-kyc-privacy) from users leaving OpenAI over its Pentagon contract. Privacy-conscious users chose Anthropic as the safer alternative. Now some of those same users are being asked to hand over a selfie and government document.

Anthropic says verification data is handled by a third-party firm (Persona), stored separately from Anthropic's systems, and won't be used for model training or marketing. The verification is reportedly targeted, not universal — but "targeted" still means some users will hit the wall.

ChatGPT and Gemini don't currently require this. Anthropic hasn't pointed to any regulatory mandate pushing them toward KYC. The company's stated goal is abuse prevention, but the optics for a company built on Constitutional AI and safety-first messaging are rough.
